Beef Stir Fry

Description

Enjoy the mild, aromatic flavours of India in this easy meal. Tender strips of chicken get a light tandoori coating, which goes perfectly with fragrant garlic rice. A collection of zesty sides, including mint yoghurt and mango chutney, take this dinner to the next level.

Ingredients

  • 2 cloves
  • 1 packet chicken breast
  • 1 bunch mint
  • 1 punnet cheery tomatoes
  • 1 packet basmati rice
  • 1 tub tandoori paste
  • 1 packet greek-style yoghurt
  • 1 bag baby spinach leaves
  • 20g butter
  • 1.5 cup water
  • 1 tsp white wine vinegar
  • Â½ tsp salt
  • olive oil

Steps

  1. Finely chop the garlic. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter with a dash of olive oil over a medium-high heat. Add the garlic and cook until golden and fragrant, 1-2 minutes. Add the basmati rice, water (for the rice) and 1/2 the salt, stir, and bring to the boil. Reduce the heat to low and cover with a lid. Cook for 10 minutes, then remove from the heat and keep covered until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed, 10 minutes.
  2. While the rice is cooking, slice the chicken breast into 1cm strips. In a medium bowl, combine the tandoori paste, remaining salt and a good drizzle of olive oil. Add the chicken strips and toss to coat.
  3. Pick and finely chop the mint leaves. In a small bowl, combine the Greek yoghurt and 1/2 the mint. Season with salt and pepper and mix well. Set aside.
  4. Slice the cherry tomatoes in half. Roughly chop the baby spinach leaves. In a second medium bowl, combine the cherry tomatoes, spinach and remaining mint. Add the white wine vinegar and drizzle with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper and stir to combine.
  5. In a large frying pan, heat a drizzle of olive oil over a high heat. When the oil is hot, cook the tandoori chicken, in batches, tossing, until the chicken is browned and cooked through, 4-5 minutes.
  6. Divide the garlic rice and chicken tikka between bowls. Serve with the mint yoghurt and tomato salad
